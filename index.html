Early Life and Education

Bozoma Saint John was born on April 21, 1977, in Watertown, Massachusetts, to Ghanaian parents. Growing up in a multicultural environment, she was exposed to a blend of rich cultural heritage and diverse perspectives. Her father was a prominent Ghanaian advocate and her mother was an accomplished administrator. This diverse upbringing played a crucial role in shaping her worldview and work ethic.

She attended the University of California, Berkeley, where she earned a bachelor’s degree in English. Later, she pursued an MBA at the MIT Sloan School of Management. This combination of a liberal arts education and a strong business foundation equipped her with unique skills that would prove invaluable in her later career.
